Five Things That Have Changed Over Time

Key transformations in the history of Cali — present perfect

01
Urban Growth
The city has expanded far beyond its original borders

Cali has grown from a small colonial settlement founded in 1536 into one of Colombia's largest metropolitan areas, transforming lush valleys and steep hillsides into vibrant, densely populated urban neighborhoods.

02
Cultural Identity
Salsa has become the beating heart of the city

Since the mid-20th century, salsa music has woven itself deeply into the soul of Cali, earning the city its legendary worldwide title as the World Capital of Salsa.

03
Public Transport
The MIO system has transformed how people move

The Masivo Integrado de Occidente has replaced the old chaotic bus network with a modern Bus Rapid Transit system, connecting millions of passengers across the city every single day.

04
Economy
Industry and commerce have reshaped the local economy

Over the decades, Cali has shifted from an agrarian sugar-cane economy to a diversified industrial and services hub, and the Cauca Valley has become one of Colombia's most productive economic corridors.

05
Sports Legacy
International games have put Cali on the world map

Since hosting the 1971 Pan American Games, Cali has built a world-class sports infrastructure and has established itself as a premier destination for major international athletic competitions across Latin America.
